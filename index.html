Ravens From Yesterday

Three battles were fought. Some houses feasted. Dion set his own tent on fire.

"When you play the game of brackets, you win or you pick DR Congo."

The morning opened with a scare. England 2, DR Congo 1 saw Cipenga stun the Three Lions inside seven minutes, and for a brief, beautiful stretch ten of you sweated into your breakfast. Harry Kane, ever the party pooper, headed in twice down the stretch to rescue the tenth-most-popular pick in the building. Ten houses breathed out. And then there was Dion, alone in the wilderness, who looked at England versus DR Congo and boldly backed Congo. Bold. Wrong, but bold. Zero points, and the ravens are still laughing.

The afternoon was the bloodbath. Belgium 3, Senegal 2 (a.e.t.) was pure Red Wedding energy. Senegal led 2-0, cups were half-raised, and then Lukaku headed one back at 86', Tielemans levelled at 89', and in the 125th minute (the latest goal in World Cup history, look it up) Tielemans buried a penalty to send Senegal home. Eight of you had Belgium and collected. Travis, Dion and Mike had Senegal, and paid for it. For Mike it was more than five points. It was the last nail. More on that grim news below.

Then, in the evening, the one bit of peace. United States 2, Bosnia 0 at Levi's Stadium was the single match every soul in this pool called correctly. Balogun struck on the stroke of half, Tillman salted it away, and eleven brackets nodded in smug unison. Five points for the realm, no drama, no heroes. Enjoy the memory, because it was the last time you all agreed on anything.

Day's damage: Karly, Adam, John, Kim, Alex, Tommy, Sean and Art banked the full +15. Travis and Mike took +10. And Dion, betrayed by both of his brave contrarian hearts, limped off with a lonely +5.
